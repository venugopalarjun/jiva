Install with one commandRecommended
$ curl -fsSL https://jiva.works/install.sh | bash
Downloads Jiva, verifies its checksum, installs it to your Applications, and opens it — no drag, no Gatekeeper prompt. Safe to re-run anytime; it skips the download if you're already current.
Prefer Homebrew?
$ brew install --cask venugopalarjun/jiva/jiva
Or by hand: use the Download for Mac button, drag Jiva to Applications, then run xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/Jiva.app once in Terminal before opening it.
100% local No cloud No account

Everything, on-device

The whole pipeline runs locally — your audio never touches a server.

Live transcription

Both sides of the call, transcribed on-device as you talk.

Speaker memory

Labels who said what — and recognizes the same voice across every meeting, so names stick instead of “Speaker 1.”

Live assist overlay

A private glass overlay over any app: a running summary, one-tap Assist for what to say next, and a box to ask your past meetings — invisible to screen share.

Hold-to-talk dictation

Hold a key, speak anywhere, and it types for you.

Action items, automatically

Decisions and to-dos are pulled out for you — each linked to the exact moment it was said.

Auto-record

Starts the moment a meeting begins, stops when it ends.

Cited, not invented

Every summary, decision, and answer links back to who said it and when — tap a citation to jump to that moment.
